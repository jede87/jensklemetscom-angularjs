how to brew beer

why brew your own?

  • you will start appreciating beer in a new way

  • you will want to know more about beers

  • you will notice that your homebrew is better than most beers

is it worth it?

yes.

a typical brew day

time:

task:

9:00

get brewing gear from the basement preparation + equipment

9:08

boil 1 liters of water for rehydration of the yeast. put the kettle aside until the yeast need to be preparared. how to prepare a yeast

9:10

put on some music. i have done my best beers when playing this list on spotify: hip hop essentials by looptroop rockers

9:17

clean the kitchen. spray all surfaces with sanitation spray to minimize the risk of an infected beer. sanitation

9:25

wash the brewing kettle first, so that you can start heating the water for mashing while cleaning up other equipment. singel temperature infusion

9:30

take the yeast from the fridge and put it on the kitchen counter so it reaches room temperature before rehydration how to prepare a yeast

9:33

fill the brewing kettle with 18l of the hottest water that you can get from the tap then wait until the water will reach 72°C. i use a short hose connected to the tap to make it easier to fill up the brewing kettle

9:40

put the false bottom in the brewing kettle. rinse the grain bag and put it on the brewing kettle

9:43

wash stirring spoon

9:46

prepare grains by opening your grain bags and/or weigh the grains according to recipe

9:55

when water reaches 72°C lower stove heat to 6 (induction) and add all grain to the brewing kettle while stirring. lower stove heat to 2. check temperature. at this point it should be around 67°C. if not, add warm water to increase temperature. let mash for 1 hour. try to keep temperature around 67°. check the temperature every 15min. starting the mash

9:56

wash fermenter and drainer

9:59

brew coffee how to brew coffee using a cafetiere

10:06

boil water in the 5 liter kettle. this will be used as sparge water aspects of lautering

10:15

drink coffee how to enjoy coffee again

10:16

wash all unwashed equipment

10:23

prepare grain bag drain station. put a layer of old newspapers under and around the fermenter.

10:59

start mash out with raising the temperature of the brewing kettle to 76°C. also check that the temperature of the spareg water is around 76°C

11:07

lift grain bag out of the kettle and put it in the drainer on top of the fermenter. pour sparge water in the grain bag when you feel comfortable to let go of the bag. use some sort of clip to keep the bag closed so it can drip alone

11:28

pour the wort from the fermenter to the brew kettle and add more of the hottest water from the tap so that amount of wort is 25 liters

11:33

start boiling the wort in the brewing kettle. it will take a while before it reaches the boiling temeprature

11:35

wash and sanitize the fermenter and the fermenters lid

11:45

weigh hops and put them in small cups in the correct order according to the recipe

12:00

when the wort in the brewing kettle have started to boil add hops according to recipe

13:07

cool the wort as fast as possible until it reaches room temperature cooling the wort

13:15

start preparing the yeast. check that the temperature of the boiled water is between 30 - 41 celsius. dissolve yeast in sanitized container. preparing yeast and yeast starters

13:28

move wort to fermenter and aeriate it properly oxygen, aeration is good oxidation is bad

13:30

taste beer and measure OG how much alcohol will there be

13:39

pitch the rehydrated yeast to the wort in the fermenter

13:41

put the lid on and prepare air lock with vodka or water with starsan

13:45

clean up the kitchen

14:00

done!
